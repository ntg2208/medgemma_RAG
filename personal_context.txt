## 5. ACTIVE CLINICAL PRIORITIES (March 2026)

### URGENT — Potassium: Critically Low

- **Problem:** Pre-dialysis potassium below target; post-dialysis dangerously low
- **Mechanism:** Augmented 4×/week home HD schedule drives highly efficient potassium clearance. Combined with previously restrictive diet, this has caused hypokalaemia.
- **Clinical significance:** Hypokalaemia increases risk of cardiac arrhythmias (QT prolongation), muscle weakness, and cardiac arrest
- **Intervention 1 — Dialysate potassium increase (3 → 4 mmol/L):** This is the correct clinical lever and is an **outstanding urgent discussion item** with the renal team. Reducing dialysate volume would compromise clearance of urea, creatinine, phosphate, and middle molecules.
- **Intervention 2 — Dietary potassium increase:** Increase intake of high-potassium, low-phosphorus foods distributed across meals in moderate portions. Large bolus potassium loads cause mild chest pain (a real physiological phenomenon without renal buffering capacity).
- **Guideline reference:** UKRA HD Guideline 5.1 — individualised dialysate potassium using the highest concentration sufficient to control pre-dialysis levels; target pre-dialysis K 4.0–6.0 mmol/L

### URGENT — Folic Acid Deficiency

- **Level:** 2.6 µg/L (low)
- **Impact:** Likely contributing to declining platelet trend; impairs DNA synthesis in megakaryocytes
- **Action:** Folic acid 5 mg daily — urgent prescription to pursue with clinical team

### HIGH PRIORITY — Phosphate: Elevated Above Target

- **Target range:** 1.1–1.7 mmol/L (UKRA HD Guideline)
- **Mechanism:** Loss of convective HDF clearance since transitioning to standard home HD; dietary phosphate intake
- **Key principle — Inorganic vs organic phosphate:** Inorganic phosphate additives (E338–E343, E450–E452) have ~90–100% absorption vs ~40–60% for organic phosphate from whole foods. Phytate-bound phosphorus in plant sources (e.g., tofu) absorbs even less. Label checking for E-numbers is essential.
- **Sevelamer timing:** Must be taken at START of meals to bind phosphate while food is still digesting
- **Sample artefact:** Blood sample transport delays (5 hrs post-dialysis, 9 hrs pre-dialysis) artefactually raise phosphate readings — true levels may be closer to target than reported

- **Recent value:** 111 × 10⁹/L (down from 181 × 10⁹/L)
- **Contributing factors:** (1) Uraemic toxin-mediated platelet dysfunction, (2) Folic acid deficiency, (3) Possible artefactual lowering from EDTA-dependent pseudothrombocytopenia due to sample transport delays
- **Investigation needed:** Blood film + citrate tube recheck to distinguish true thrombocytopenia from artefact

## 8. DIETARY FRAMEWORK

### Core Principles

**The potassium–phosphorus tension:** Many high-potassium foods also carry high phosphate. The dietary sweet spot is foods with strongly favourable K:P ratios.

### High-Potassium, Low-Phosphorus Foods (Priority)
- **Vegetables:** White potato, sweet potato, courgette, green beans, fresh peas
- **Fruits:** Kiwi, orange, mango, melon (honeydew), coconut water
- **Cooking method matters:** Baking/roasting retains more potassium than boiling (which leaches it into water)

### Protein Sources (Ranked by Suitability)
1. **Chicken breast** — best overall K:P ratio and bioavailability
2. **Tofu** — supplementary; phytate-bound phosphorus absorbs poorly (favourable)
3. **Fresh white fish** — must be purchased fresh, cooked immediately, frozen rather than refrigerated (histamine management)
4. **Unprocessed pork** — acceptable
5. **Beef** — occasional only
6. **Egg whites** — high-efficiency protein with near-zero phosphorus; whole eggs acceptable in moderation
7. **Unflavoured whey protein isolate (WPI, not WPC)** — suitable supplement; check for phosphate additive E-numbers

### Phosphate Additives to Avoid
- **E338–E343** (phosphoric acid family)
- **E450–E452** (polyphosphates)
- These have ~90–100% absorption vs 40–60% for organic phosphate
- Essential to check labels on all processed/packaged foods

### Histamine Considerations (Revised)
- **Strict histamine avoidance is NO LONGER indicated** — doctor confirmed fistula itching is tape/adhesive contact dermatitis, not systemic histamine
- Fermented ingredients (gochujang, small amounts of fish sauce, tomatoes, butternut squash) are acceptable in normal cooking quantities with moderation
- Korean and Vietnamese fermented ingredients are back in diet
- Fish: Purchase fresh, cook immediately, freeze leftovers rather than refrigerating

### Cooking & Storage Practices
- All meals prepared from scratch at home (batch cooking)
- **Cook → portion → freeze immediately** protocol (prevents histamine accumulation in stored food)
- Soups and cooked dishes frozen promptly rather than refrigerated for multiple days

### Cultural Preferences
- Strong preference for Korean and Vietnamese cuisine
- Uses gochujang, fish sauce in cooking (now permitted with moderation)

## 9. KEY CLINICAL LEARNINGS & PRINCIPLES

### Dialysis Adequacy
- Augmented 4×/week schedule efficiently clears small molecules (urea, creatinine, potassium) but does NOT provide convective clearance of middle molecules (β2-microglobulin, PTH) the way HDF does
- Previous in-centre HDF provided superior convective clearance — loss of this contributes to rising PTH and uremic symptoms
- **Hybrid schedule** (home HD + periodic in-centre HDF for middle molecule clearance) is clinically rational
- Dialysate potassium concentration is the correct lever for managing hypokalaemia — NOT reducing dialysate volume (which would compromise overall clearance)

### Sample Transport & Lab Artefacts
- Transport delays artefactually raise: phosphate, potassium
- Transport delays artefactually lower: bicarbonate, platelet counts (EDTA-dependent pseudothrombocytopenia)
- True potassium may be even lower than reported dangerously low values
- Cool storage and faster lab processing recommended

### Buttonhole Cannulation
- Week-to-week resistance variation is normal
- Cumulative granulation tissue at the vessel wall junction (not technique failure) is the primary cause of resistance after prolonged use
- Fundamentals are sound if nurses only need to add force rather than correct positioning

### Positional Management
- Elevating ankles to match head height neutralises the hydrostatic pressure column effect that worsens capillary leaking in the context of impaired platelet function (relevant for petechiae)

### Pain Management
- Paracetamol for pain management (NSAIDs contraindicated in ESRD context)
